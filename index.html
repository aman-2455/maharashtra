THE MAHARASHTRA

Maharashtra is a prominent state in western India, occupying a significant part of the Deccan Plateau. It is the third-largest state in India by area (307,713 sq km) and the second-most populous, with over 112 million people according to the 2011 census. The state is bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west and shares boundaries with several states, including Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Telangana, Karnataka, and Goa. The capital, Mumbai, is India's largest city and serves as the nation's financial and commercial hub, housing key institutions like the Reserve Bank of India and the Bombay Stock Exchange. Other major cities include Pune, a leading IT hub, and Nagpur, which serves as the winter capital. Maharashtra is renowned for its diverse geography, featuring the Western Ghats (Sahyadri Range), the Konkan coastal plains, and the fertile Maharashtra is a leader in both agricultural and industrial production. While nearly half of its population is engaged in agriculture, the sector contributes only about 12% to the state's GDP, with rice, millet, sugarcane, and cotton being major crops. The service sector, including finance, IT, tourism, and creative industries, dominates the state's economy, accounting for 70% of its output. The state also has a vibrant film industry, with Mumbai being the heart of Bollywood. Culturally, Maharashtra is known for its rich heritage, historic sites, and festivals. The official language is Marathi, spoken by about 70% of the population. The state boasts six UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, and the Chhatrapati Shivaj
