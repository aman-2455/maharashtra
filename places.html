Raigad

  1. Raigad Fort, located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, India, is one of the most iconic and historically significant forts in Indian history. Perched at an elevation of approximately 2,700 feet (820 meters) above sea level, it served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under the legendary ruler Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Originally known as "Rairi," the fort was captured by Shivaji Maharaj in 1656 from the More clan, who had initially constructed it. After extensive renovations and fortifications, Shivaji renamed it Raigad, meaning "Royal Fort," and made it his capital in 1674.
  2. Architecturally, Raigad Fort showcases the engineering and military ingenuity of the 17th century. The massive wooden doors studded with iron, the thick stone walls, and the bastions were designed to withstand prolonged sieges. The fort also contains hidden water cisterns that ensured a sustainable water supply during conflicts.

Tajhotel

  1. The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel is a heritage, five-star luxury hotel located in the Colaba area of Mumbai, India, adjacent to the Gateway of India. It was commissioned by Jamsetji Tata and opened on 16 December 1903. The hotel was built in the Indo-Saracenic architectural style and named after the Taj Mahal in Agra, although the two are separate landmarks about 1,050 km apart.
  2. It was one of the main targets during the 2008 Mumbai terrorist attacks, suffering significant damage and loss of life but was restored and reopened later that year. The hotel is considered the flagship property of the Taj Hotels group, employing around 1,600 staff and offering around 560 rooms and 44 suites
